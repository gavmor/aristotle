# Joe Sachs

Translator of the edition of the [[references/nicomachean-ethics]] this wiki's pages are drawn from, and a tutor at St. John's College, Annapolis. He has also translated Aristotle's *De Anima*, *Poetics*, and other works in the same idiom.

## Key Ideas

- Argues, in his preface, that the standard English translation tradition for Aristotle is "infected by a long Latin tradition" that obscures the everyday-speech roots of Aristotle's technical vocabulary — in particular rendering *hexis* as "habit" or "state," *energeia* as "activity," and *to kalon* as "noble" or "fine," each of which he considers a serious mistranslation. ^[extracted]
- His introduction ("Three Little Words") argues that habit, the mean, and the beautiful are the three terms in the Ethics most prone to being misunderstood, and offers his own reconstruction of each: character (not habit) is what makes virtue possible; the mean is a qualitative stand between obstacles, not a quantitative midpoint; and "the beautiful" (*to kalon*) is the actual end for the sake of which virtuous people act. ^[extracted]
- Reads the Ethics as building toward the conclusion, via Books VIII-IX, that friendship — not justice — is Aristotle's real answer for what holds a political community together, since Aristotle treats justice as "inherently inadequate" on its own terms. ^[inferred]
