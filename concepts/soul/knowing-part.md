# Knowing Part (Epistēmonikon)

The knowing part (sometimes translated as the scientific part) is one of the two subdivisions of the [[concepts/soul/rational-part|rational soul]] (Book VI, Chapter 1). 

## Key Ideas

- **Invariable Objects**: It is dedicated strictly to contemplating things whose governing principles are [[concepts/incapable-of-being-otherwise|incapable of being otherwise]] (i.e., eternal, necessary truths). 
- **Truth-Disclosing Powers**: It houses the intellectual virtues of **[[concepts/nous|intellect]]** (grasping first principles), **knowledge** (*epistēmē* - demonstrating from principles), and **[[concepts/sophia|wisdom]]** (the perfection of both).

### Bk. VI, ch. 1 (Bekker 1139a11-12)

```ngloss
\ex λεγέσθω δὲ τούτων τὸ μὲν ἐπιστημονικὸν τὸ δὲ λογιστικόν·
\gl λεγέσθω [legesthō] [let-be-called]
    δὲ [de] [and]
    τούτων [toutōn] [of-these.GEN]
    τὸ [to] [the.NOM]
    μὲν [men] [one.PTCL]
    ἐπιστημονικὸν [epistēmonikon] [knowing-part.NOM]
    τὸ [to] [the.NOM]
    δὲ [de] [other.PTCL]
    λογιστικόν· [logistikon] [calculating-part.NOM]
\ft And let one of these be called the knowing part, and the other the calculating part;
```

This sentence introduces the fundamental bifurcation of the rational soul based on the objects it contemplates. The term *epistēmonikon* derives directly from *epistēmē* (knowledge/science), denoting the faculty built for grasping unchangeable truths.
