# Calculating Part (Logistikon)

The calculating or deliberative part (*logistikon*) is the subdivision of the [[concepts/soul/rational-part|rational soul]] aimed at practical affairs (Book VI, Chapter 1).

## Key Ideas

- **Variable Objects**: Unlike the knowing part, the calculating part contemplates things [[concepts/capable-of-being-otherwise|capable of being otherwise]]—contingent events, human actions, and production.
- **Deliberation and Choice**: It is responsible for deliberation, which ultimately issues in [[concepts/prohairesis|choice]].
- **Truth-Disclosing Powers**: It houses the intellectual virtues of **[[concepts/phronesis|practical judgment]]** (directed toward action) and **art** (*technē* - directed toward making).

### Bk. VI, ch. 1 (Bekker 1139a11-12)

```ngloss
\ex λεγέσθω δὲ τούτων τὸ μὲν ἐπιστημονικὸν τὸ δὲ λογιστικόν·
\gl λεγέσθω [legesthō] [let-be-called]
    δὲ [de] [and]
    τούτων [toutōn] [of-these.GEN]
    τὸ [to] [the.NOM]
    μὲν [men] [one.PTCL]
    ἐπιστημονικὸν [epistēmonikon] [knowing-part.NOM]
    τὸ [to] [the.NOM]
    δὲ [de] [other.PTCL]
    λογιστικόν· [logistikon] [calculating-part.NOM]
\ft And let one of these be called the knowing part, and the other the calculating part;
```

This sentence introduces the fundamental bifurcation of the rational soul based on the objects it contemplates. The term *logistikon* is related to *logizesthai* (to calculate or deliberate), marking this as the faculty of practical reasoning and deliberation about variable things.
