# Irrational Part (Alogon)

The irrational or non-rational part of the [[concepts/soul/soul|soul]] (*alogon*) is the macro-domain encompassing all biological and emotional drives that do not formulate reason on their own.

## Key Ideas

- **The Vegetative / Nutritive Part**: Completely deaf to reason, this part governs growth and nutrition. Aristotle excludes it from human moral consideration because it is shared with plants.
- **The Appetitive / Desiring Part**: Though lacking reason in itself, this part can "listen to and obey" reason, much like a child obeying a parent (Book I, Chapter 13). This capacity for obedience makes it the seat of the virtues of character.
- **Sense-Perception**: An irrational capacity shared with animals that provides awareness but originates no deliberate human action (Book VI, Chapter 2).

Here, Aristotle formally recaps his bipartite division from Book I before further subdividing the rational part. The word *alogon* literally means "without reason" or "without speech," encompassing everything from biological processes to emotional desires.
