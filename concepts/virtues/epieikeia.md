# Epieikeia (Decency / Equity)

In Aristotle's *Nicomachean Ethics* (Book V), **Epieikeia** is the virtue of decency or equity. It is deeply related to justice, serving as a corrective to legally codified justice. Because laws must be written as universal rules, they cannot account for every unique, particular situation. The decent or equitable person (*epieikēs*) is one who recognizes when the rigid application of the law would actually produce an unjust result, and voluntarily takes less than their strict legal rights in order to preserve the *spirit* of justice.

Aristotle defines decency (or equity, *epieikeia*) not as a virtue entirely distinct from justice, but as a specific, superior *kind* of justice that corrects the rigid universality of written law. The decent person does not strictly stand on their legal rights when doing so would cause harm (*akribodikaios epi to cheiron*); this active condition (*hexis*) of taking less than one's legal due is, ultimately, what true justice looks like in complex human situations.
