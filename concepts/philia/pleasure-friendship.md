# Friendship for Pleasure

In Book VIII, Chapters 2–3 of the *Nicomachean Ethics*, Aristotle describes **friendship for pleasure** as the second incidental (*kata symbebēkos*) species of friendship.

## Key Ideas

- **Incidental Basis:** The bond is based on the immediate enjoyment (*hēdonē*) or amusement the other person provides, rather than their intrinsic character.
- **Prevalence Among the Young:** Young people live according to feeling (*pathos*) and pursue what is pleasant at the moment; hence, their friendships form quickly and end quickly as tastes change.
- **Erotic Relationships:** Erotic passion (*erōtikē philia*) is largely of this kind; it fluctuates rapidly with changes in appearance, mood, and attraction.
- **Transient Nature:** Like utility friendships, pleasure friendships dissolve whenever the source of pleasure fades or the friends' desires alter.
