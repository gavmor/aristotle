# Likemindedness / Concord (Homonoia)

In Book IX, Chapter 6 of the *Nicomachean Ethics*, Aristotle analyzes **concord** or **likemindedness** (*homonoia*), identifying it as political friendship (*politikē philia*).

## Key Ideas

- **Not Merely Agreement in Opinion:** *Homonoia* is not simply sharing the same belief about abstract or theoretical matters (such as astronomical phenomena).
- **Agreement in Action & Governance:** Concord occurs when citizens agree about **practical affairs**, choose the same policies, and execute decisions made in common (e.g., agreeing that offices should be elective or that an alliance should be formed).
- **Possessed by Decent People (*Epieikeis*):** True concord is possible only among decent people, who are in agreement both with themselves and with one another, standing on the same ground (*epi tōn autōn ontōn*). 
- **Faction (*Stasis*) as Deficiency:** Base people cannot achieve lasting concord except for brief moments, because each strives to grab more than his share of advantages while avoiding burdens, leading to civil strife (*stasis*).
