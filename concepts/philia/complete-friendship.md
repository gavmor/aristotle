# Complete Friendship (Virtue Friendship)

In Books VIII and IX of the *Nicomachean Ethics*, Aristotle presents **complete friendship** (*teleia philia*) as the true archetype of friendship, of which utility and pleasure friendships are only partial imitations.

## Key Ideas

- **Based on Virtue and Character:** Formed between good people (*agathoi*) who are alike in virtue (*kat' aretēn*). Each wishes good things for the other for the other's own sake (*ekeinou heneka*).
- **Enduring & Stable:** Because virtue is a settled, enduring active condition (*hexis*), complete friendship is lasting and immune to slander or sudden change.
- **Includes Utility & Pleasure as Byproducts:** Complete friends are also unconditionally useful and pleasant to one another, because a good person's actions are pleasant and beneficial to another good person.
- **Rare & Requires Time:** Such friendships are rare because good people are few, and because they require time, shared living (*suzēn*), and long testing ("consuming the proverbial salt together").
- **A Friend as "Another Self" (*Allos Autos*):** In complete friendship, the friend is experienced as an extension of one's own virtuous self, allowing one to observe and participate in virtuous activity through another.
