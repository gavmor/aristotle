# The -eia Suffix (Abstract Nouns of Condition and Activity)

In Ancient Greek, **`-εια`** (*-eia*) is a fundamental **abstract-noun-forming suffix** attached to adjectival or verbal stems. It denotes **"the state, quality, or active condition of being [X]"** (equivalent to English *-ness*, *-ty*, or *-hood*). 

In the *Nicomachean Ethics*, Aristotle relies heavily on `-εια` nouns to define key ethical states, moral virtues, psychological conditions, and political structures.

---

## 1. Philosophical & Ontological Foundations

- **[[concepts/energeia|ἐνέργεια]] (*energeia*, 114x):** *Being-at-work / Activity*. Fused from `ἐν` ("in") + `ἔργον` ("work") + `-εια`. Coined by Aristotle himself to name an ongoing, fully realized state of activity complete at every moment.
- **[[concepts/justice/epieikeia|ἐπιείκεια]] (*epieikeia*, 6x):** *Decency / Equity*. From `ἐπί` ("upon") + `εἰκός` ("fitting/reasonable") + `-εια`. The active condition of correcting the rigid letter of universal law.
- **`αὐτάρκεια` (*autarkeia*, 3x):** *Self-sufficiency*. From `αὐτός` ("self") + `ἀρκέω` ("to suffice") + `-εια`. The condition of lacking nothing necessary for a choiceworthy life.

---

## 2. Virtues & Active Conditions (*Hexeis*)

- **[[concepts/active-conditions/andreia|ἀνδρεία]] (*andreia*, 29x):** *Courage / Manliness*. From `ἀνήρ / ἀνδρός` ("man") + `-εια`. The mean condition regarding fear and confidence, facing death in battle for the sake of the beautiful (*to kalon*).
- **[[concepts/active-conditions/aletheia|ἀλήθεια]] (*alētheia*, 22x):** *Truthfulness / Truth*. From `ἀ-` ("un-") + `λήθη` ("forgetting/hiddenness") + `-εια`. Literally "un-hiddenness," the social virtue of truthful self-presentation.
- **[[concepts/continence|ἐγκράτεια]] (*enkrateia*, 20x):** *Continence / Self-restraint*. From `ἐν` ("in") + `κράτος` ("power/mastery") + `-εια`. The inner state of holding power over one's strong base desires through reason.
- **[[concepts/active-conditions/megaloprepeia|μεγαλοπρέπεια]] (*megaloprepeia*, 7x):** *Magnificence*. From `μέγας` ("great") + `πρέπω` ("fitting/conspicuous") + `-εια`. The virtue of large-scale, noble expenditures.
- **`εἰρωνεία` (*eirōneia*, 5x):** *Irony / Understatement*. From `εἴρων` ("dissembling understater") + `-εια`. The vice of understating one's own merits (associated with Socrates).

---

## 3. Moral Psychology & Internal States

- **`μεταμέλεια` (*metameleia*, 4x):** *Remorse / Regret*. From `μετά` ("after") + `μέλω` ("to care") + `-εια`. The painful post-action remorse characteristic of involuntary or base acts.
- **`συνήθεια` (*synētheia*, 7x):** *Habituation / Intimate Association*. From `σύν` ("together") + `ἦθος` ("custom/character") + `-εια`. The shared habituation required for friendship and law.
- **`εὐλάβεια` (*eulabeia*, 1x):** *Caution / Circumspection*. From `εὖ` ("well") + `λαμβάνω` ("to grasp") + `-εια`.
- **`εὐπάθεια` (*eupatheia*, 2x):** *Good feeling / Comfort*. From `εὖ` + `πάθος` ("feeling") + `-εια`.

---

## 4. Political & Social Institutions

- **`πολιτεία` (*politeia*, 18x):** *Constitution / Polity*. From `πολίτης` ("citizen") + `-εια`. The structural ordering of offices and civic life in a city.
- **`βασιλεία` (*basileia*, 6x):** *Kingship / Monarchy*. From `βασιλεύς` ("king") + `-εια`. The noble monarchical constitution.
- **`δυναστεία` (*dynasteia*, 5x):** *Dynasty / Oligarchic Dominance*. From `δυνάστης` ("ruler") + `-εια`.
- **`δουλεία` (*douleia*, 1x):** *Servitude / Slavery*. From `δοῦλος` ("slave") + `-εια`.
- **`παιδεία` (*paideia*, 5x):** *Upbringing / Education*. From `παῖς` ("child") + `-εια`. The rearing of youth in virtue.
- **`εὐγένεια` (*eugeneia*, 2x):** *Good Birth / Noble Ancestry*. From `εὖ` + `γένος` ("family") + `-εια`.

---

## 5. Vices of Deficiency or Excess

- **`μικροπρέπεια` (*mikroprepeia*, 2x):** *Meanness / Pettiness*. The deficiency vice opposite magnificence.
- **`αἰσχροκέρδεια` (*aischrokerdeia*, 1x):** *Sordid Gain*. From `αἰσχρός` ("shameful") + `κέρδος` ("profit") + `-εια`. The petty vice of seeking shameful financial advantage.
- **`ἔνδεια` (*endeia*, 8x):** *Need / Deficiency*. From `ἐν` + `δέω` ("to lack") + `-εια`.
