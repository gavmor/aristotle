# Eugeneia (Good Birth / Noble Ancestry)

In Aristotle's treatment of social goods, **`εὐγένεια`** (*eugeneia*, good birth / noble ancestry) is the status of descending from ancient, virtuous, or distinguished lineage.

The word is an `-εια` abstract noun derived from **`εὖ`** (*eu*, "well") + **`γένος`** (*genos*, "family / birth").

---

## Key Ideas

- **An External Good / Ornament:** In Book I, ch. 8 (1099b3), Aristotle includes good birth among the external goods (*ektos agatha*)—alongside good children, beauty, and wealth—that adorn a happy life. Lacking noble birth or good children can tarnish one's blessedness (*makariotēs*). ^[extracted]
- **Relation to Greatness of Soul (*Megalopsychia*):** In Book IV, ch. 3 (1124a21), Aristotle notes that people of noble birth, power, or wealth are deemed worthy of honor by most, and that these advantages make a person more great-souled, though virtue alone is the true ground of greatness of soul. ^[extracted]
