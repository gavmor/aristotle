# Douleia (Servitude / Slavery)

In Aristotle's social analysis, **`δουλεία`** (*douleia*, servitude / slavery) is the condition of being an unfree, dependent instrument of another within the household (*oikos*).

The word is an `-εια` abstract noun derived from **`δοῦλος`** (*doulos*, "slave").

---

## Key Ideas

- **Living Tool (*Organon Empsychon*):** In Book VIII, ch. 11 (1161b3), Aristotle famously characterizes a slave as an "animated tool" (*organon empsychon*), just as a tool is an inanimate slave. ^[extracted]
- **Friendship with a Slave:** Aristotle resolves the puzzle of whether one can be friends with a slave through a subtle distinction:
  - ***Qua* Slave:** There can be no friendship or justice toward a slave *insofar as he is a slave*, because a slave and master share no common legal standing or equality.
  - ***Qua* Human Being:** Insofar as a slave is a human being capable of participating in law and contract, friendship *is* possible with him. ^[extracted]
- **Household Rule:** Servitude represents despotism (*despoteia*)—rule exercised for the benefit of the master, contrasting sharply with political rule (*politē*) exercised among free and equal citizens for the common good. ^[extracted]
