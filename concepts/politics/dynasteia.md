# Dynasteia (Dynasty / Oligarchic Dominance)

In Aristotle's political vocabulary, **`δυναστεία`** (*dynasteia*) denotes a narrow, lawless form of oligarchy where political power is monopolized by a dominant family, clique, or faction above the rule of law.

The word is an `-εια` abstract noun derived from **`δυνάστης`** (*dynastēs*, "ruler / lord / powerful person").

---

## Key Ideas

- **Oligarchic Extreme:** While moderate oligarchy allocates offices by property qualification under fixed laws, a *dynasteia* occurs when a tiny wealthy elite rules arbitrarily without legal restraint. ^[extracted]
- **Personal Rule vs. Compulsory Law:** In Book X, ch. 9, Aristotle contrasts the arbitrary, hated commands of individual powerful men (*dynastai*) with the impartial, non-resented compulsory power of **law (*nomos*)**. ^[extracted]
