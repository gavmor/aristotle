# Makarios (The Blessed Person)

In the *Nicomachean Ethics*, Aristotle distinguishes between ordinary happiness (*eudaimonia*) as an active condition and the ultimate human ideal of **blessedness** (*makarios* / *makariotēs*). The blessed person represents the full, unblemished realization of the human good.

## Key Ideas

- **Being-at-Work in Virtue and Contemplation:** The blessed person's life is defined not by passive enjoyment, but by continuous [[concepts/energeia|being-at-work]] (*energeia*) of the soul in accordance with complete virtue. While this encompasses moral virtues, supreme blessedness reaches its peak in [[concepts/contemplative-life|contemplation]] (*theōria*), the activity most like that of the divine. ^[extracted]
- **Self-Sufficiency and External Goods:** The blessed person is fundamentally self-sufficient (*autarkēs*), lacking nothing necessary for a choiceworthy life. However, Aristotle pragmatically emphasizes that blessedness requires a sufficient stock of **external goods** (friends, moderate wealth, noble ancestry, children) as equipment (*organa*) for performing noble actions. Massive external deprivation can "besmirch" (*rhypainousi*) blessedness even if inner virtue remains intact. ^[extracted]
- **Unshakable Resilience to Fortune:** A blessed person is not a "chameleon" shifting with every turn of luck. They are "flawlessly squarely centered" (*tetragōnos aneu psogou*), bearing all fortunes gracefully like a skilled general making the best use of whatever army is available. Even under great misfortune, they will never become miserable (*athelios*), because virtue prevents them from doing base things. ^[extracted]
- **The Highest Need for Friends:** Contrary to the view that a self-sufficient person needs no one, the blessed person has the greatest need for [[concepts/philia/complete-friendship|complete friends]]. Because a human is by nature political, and because the blessed person's joy comes from perceiving virtuous activity, having friends of serious worth ("another self") allows them to observe and share in virtuous living in a continuous, deeply fulfilling way. ^[extracted]
